# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Seekers and practitioners — people who sense that a pilgrimage to a sacred mountain is qualitatively different from adventure travel. Age range roughly 28–55. Internationally mobile, spiritually literate but not ostentatiously so. They are not looking for a retreat marketplace; they are looking for a world they can enter.

## Product Purpose

yakovrajuna.com is a ceremonial digital environment for The Open Sky — a series of sacred pilgrimages and mountain expeditions led by Yakov Rajuna. The site is a portal, not a catalogue. Its purpose is to slow the visitor down, invoke the atmosphere of the journeys, and lead the right person toward applying.

## Brand Personality

Architectural. Unhurried. Sovereign.

Emotional goals: the visitor should feel curiosity, depth, and a quiet pull — not urgency or FOMO. The site should feel like entering a living ceremonial space.

## Anti-references

- Startup / SaaS design patterns (Linear, Notion, Vercel-clean)
- Generic wellness / retreat marketplace (Airbnb Experiences, MindBodyGreen, Gaia)
- Spiritual cliché / guru branding (mandalas, chakras, lotus motifs, neon gradients)
- Luxury travel catalogue (generic Booking.com / Tripadvisor editorial)
- Coaching websites with photo + testimonials + 3-bullet value propositions
- Psychedelic / festival aesthetics

References: Aman Resorts (restraint, space, ceremony), National Geographic (authoritative imagery, editorial weight), Patagonia (quiet conviction), Arc'teryx (precision, terrain), Leica (expensive silence).

## Design Principles

1. **Depth over density.** Every section should feel like a chapter, not a card. Spacing is editorial — use tension and release, not uniform rhythm.
2. **Atmosphere before information.** The visitor should feel the mountain before they read its name. Images, color, and typographic weight carry meaning before copy does.
3. **Restraint is earned, not defaulted.** Blank space is intentional ceremony, not emptiness. Every element earns its presence.
4. **One world.** All pages are rooms inside the same building. Palette, type, and motion are consistent — visitors should never feel they left the site.
5. **Sacred minimalism over spiritual cliché.** The ceremonial quality comes from scale, silence, and structure — never from iconography.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Support reduced-motion. No color-only information conveyance. Form labels explicit (not placeholder-only). Focus indicators visible against dark backgrounds.
